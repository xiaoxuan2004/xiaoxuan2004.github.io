I am a Principal Investigator and Ph.D. Supervisor at the Institute of Medical Technology, Peking University. My research focuses on nuclear medicine imaging (PET/SPECT), with an emphasis on computational imaging for system development, physical correction, advanced image reconstruction, pharmacokinetic modeling, dosimetry, and AI-assisted diagnostics and therapeutics.
As a core member, I have played a pivotal role in developing landmark imaging systems, including the world's first small animal quad-modality molecular imaging system, the pioneering total-body PET scanner (EXPLORER), and the dedicated brain PET scanner for the U.S. BRAIN Initiative (NeuroEXPLORE).
I have authored over 30 papers in top-tier international SCI journals and hold 12 invention patents granted in China and the United States. I am the recipient of multiple international honors, including the SNMMI Young Investigator Award and the Alavi-Mandell Award (for the Journal of Nuclear Medicine's Best Paper). My research has been featured by prestigious outlets such as Science AAAS, Nature news, NIH.
